Implantology

Its objective is to replace lost teeth through the surgical placement of a dental implant in the maxillary or mandibular bone. Dental implants can be of different sizes, surfaces and materials. The prosthetic rehabilitations are designed on the implants that will allow restoring the masticatory, phonetic and aesthetic functions of the patient. The choice of the type of implant that is placed on the patients is made according to the diagnosis, prognosis and the interdisciplinary treatment plan between the general dentist and a specialist in surgery, periodontics or prosthodontics.

General odontology

General dentistry are treatments aimed at solve the primary problems that have to do with the mouth, teeth and other related structures. In most cases it represents the patient's first contact with the dentist. The general dentist makes the primary diagnosis of the patient, solves the problems of restoration of caries that may present and channels it appropriately to each specialist according to the patient's need.

The most common diseases that cater for general dentistry are cavities and gingivitis

Bone regenaration

For a dental implant to be successful, the quality of the implant is as important as that of the bone tissue of the maxilla or jaw where it is integrated. Without a good grip surface, it is very difficult for the implant to remain in the patient's mouth for a long time: dentists estimate that the area of bone that supports an implant must be at least 10 mm high and about 5 mm of thickness. Many people, for various reasons, do not keep the bone in an optimal state. Luckily, implantologists have access to bone regeneration techniques, which allow a large majority of patients to have a dental implant.

Pediatric Dentistry

Pediatric dentistry is the branch of dentistry that treats children. The pediatric dentist will, therefore, be in charge of exploring and treating children and newborns. It is also responsible for detecting possible anomalies in the position of the jaws or teeth to refer to the orthodontist, specialist in orthodontics, and to make a restorative treatment if needed. The restorative treatment mainly consists of treating the traumatisms, using sealants that have the function of slightly filling the grooves and fissures of the teeth without hardly removing dental material to avoid possible decay, and in treating the caries produced and its consequences.

The main difference between regular dentistry and pediatric dentistry in the treatment of caries is the presence of temporary or milk teeth in children which causes the treatment to change, so that the lesions that occurred in the temporary dentition will be treated less conservative and more aggressive way than those that occurred in permanent teeth, to avoid that, in the worst case, an inadequate treatment could be given to a temporary tooth that would later affect its successor.

Orthodontics

Orthodontics is a specialty of dentistry that is responsible for the study, prevention, diagnosis and treatment of anomalies in the shape, position, relationship and function of dentomaxillofacial structures. Its exercise is the art of preventing, diagnosing and correcting its possible alterations and keeping them in an optimal state of health and harmony through the use and control of different types of forces. The eagerness to correct the clenched, irregular or protruding teeth is very old because there is evidence that already in the VIII century a.C. it was trying to solve such anomalies. Among the ethnographic findings of the Greek and Etruscan world are orthodontic appliances of magnificent design.

Periodontics

It is the specialty that studies the prevention diagnosis and treatment of diseases of the gums or tissues that support the teeth. The main periodontal diseases are Gingivitis and Periodontitis. Periodontitis is the leading cause of tooth loss in the world population. It is a bacterial disease that affects the gums and the bone that supports the teeth. It can be manifested by inflammation, redness and bleeding of the gums when brushing. It can also cause bad breath and tooth mobility and loss. The treatment consists of eliminating the bacteria that cause gingivitis and / or periodontitis stopping its progression and regenerating the bone at times.

Surgery

Minimally invasive surgery

With this technique we can place dental implants without taking off the gum, considerably reducing the inflammation and discomfort of the surgery and does not need stitches.

Implant surgery

Technically, the implants are a set of medical titanium that must be placed in the bone of the maxilla or jaw. Titanium is a metal that has as a characteristic its osseo-ntegración and its good acceptance by the organism. It can be used in the vast majority of patients after study by clinical history, exploration and radiological tests.

Periodontal surgery

It aims to treat gum diseases by restoring lost structures through biomaterials, recovering lost anatomy.
